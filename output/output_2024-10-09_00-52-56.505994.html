The transition from being a student to a professional, be it for an internship or a full-time job, can be a daunting experience given the difference in environment and expectations.

This journey can be made easier if young professionals are able to find a workplace mentor who can share valuable insights and experiences that help them navigate the challenges faced in their careers.

Join us for this virtual session if you are keen to find out how mentors can support you in setting and achieving career goals, overcoming obstacles at the workplace, and potentially widen your professional network.

In this session you will:

  • Gain an understanding of the role of a mentor, and how to identify and seek a mentor at the workplace
  • Learn about the advantages and importance of a mentor for guidance and professional growth
  • Be equipped with the communication skills and etiquette to engage a mentor at the workplace

Congratulations! You have been offered an internship or a job. Now what?

After receiving a job offer, you will have to evaluate and manage the offer while considering various factors and possible scenarios. You will need to know your motivators, as well as the things to look out for in an employment contract and remuneration package. The next step is to accept or reject the offer professionally and ethically and apply appropriate negotiation techniques to achieve the best package.

In this interactive workshop, expert trainer Clement Ong will guide you in navigating the complexities of managing job offers and salary negotiation through case studies, small group discussions and facilitated reflection. This session will include live discussions in the main room and breakout rooms, where you will work on a case study, respond with your solutions and will be given live expert feedback.

In this workshop, you will learn to:

  • Identify your motivators and effectively evaluate employment contracts and remuneration packages
  • Professionally and ethically handle multiple job offers, mastering the essential Dos and Don’ts
  • Learn the Dos and Don’ts in salary negotiation and develop effective responses to salary-related inquiries
  • Understand different types of negotiation tips and techniques
  • Learn the art of gracefully accepting or declining an offer in a professional manner
  • Cultivate the ability to handle rejections with grace and professionalism

Who should attend?

  • Students who are currently applying and receiving offers for internships or full-time roles

Spaces are limited for this session and non-Business School students are given priority. For any Business School students interested to register, please register here for the waiting list.

Spaces are limited for this session and undergraduate students are given priority booking. For any Postgraduate student interested to register, please register here for the waiting list.

To foster an engaging learning environment, you will be required to activate your camera and interact with other participants. We kindly ask you to register only if you can participate fully in the session.

This two-part workshop series will provide students with an understanding of debt and equity markets and their application in financing real estate acquisitions and developments. Students will be exposed to key real estate investing topics ranging from deal sourcing, real estate acquisition, valuation and financing.

Through the workshops, you will get an overview of the following:

  • Process and tools used to identify real estate investment opportunities
  • Resources and steps required to acquire and develop an asset
  • Methods to value a real estate asset
  • How to obtain financing to acquire the asset

Who should attend:

  • Students who are interested in Private Equity, Asset Management, Investment Banking, Commercial Real Estate Finance and Brokerage

This is a two-part workshop series. While it is not mandatory to attend both workshops, we strongly recommend that students do so to maximise your learning.

Speaker

Cedric Bobo
CEO and Founder of Project Destined

Cedric Bobo is the CEO and Founder of Project Destined, a social impact vehicle that trains students to become owners and stakeholders in the communities in which they live, work and play. The platform has trained 10,000+ students across 2,000 academic institutions since 2016 and is backed by 250+ real estate firms.

Prior to founding Project Destined, Cedric spent over 20 years as an investor and investment banker including over 10 years at The Carlyle Group where he committed over $2 Billion of equity capital. Prior to Carlyle, he worked at D.L.J. Merchant Banking (London) and McCown DeLeeuw.

Cedric received his MBA from Harvard Business School and a BSME, summa cum laude from the University of Tennessee.
