The Singapore Valley Awards (SVA) is an annual entrepreneurship award programme dedicated to empowering students with promising business ideas. SVA offers fully-sponsored internships in China, providing an immersive experience in the Chinese market and real-world entrepreneurship. Established in early 2017 by a group of entrepreneurs with China-centric businesses and supported by Singapore-based private equity firm Tembusu Partners, SVA's primary mission is to foster innovation and entrepreneurship among undergraduates in Singapore.

Position Description:

We are seeking passionate and ambitious students to join our SVA internship programme. As an SVA intern, you will embark on a journey to explore the vibrant world of entrepreneurship, gain invaluable industry knowledge, and work alongside the founders of successful internet companies, corporate leaders, and experienced venture capitalists. This is an opportunity to immerse yourself in some of China's top companies, many of which have achieved global recognition.

Internship will take place between January and May 2024 with a minimum commitment period of 3 months. Singapore Citizens can receive up to 6 months’ internship visa application support via Business China's YES programme.

Selection Process:

Apply via the SVA link, selected individuals will be invited to pitch their business ideas to our panel of judges.

Qualifications:

Enrollment: You must be a currently enrolled undergraduate student in Singapore.
Passion: A genuine passion for entrepreneurship and innovation.
Skills: Strong communication and problem-solving skills.
Teamwork: Ability to work effectively in a collaborative team environment.

Greetings from Business China Youth Forum Organising Committee!

The Business China Youth Chapter (BCYC) warmly invites NUS students & staff to participate in the Business China Youth Forum 2023. The title of our upcoming forum is “New Horizons: Trailblazers Shaping the Pathways of Tomorrow”, and we are expecting a diverse group of youths from Singapore, China and the region to discuss up-and-rising opportunities and challenges in our region.

Business China Youth Forum (BCYF) has been organised in tandem with the FutureChina Global Forum (FCGF) since 2019 by the BCYC members. It aims to bring together youths in the ASEAN-China region to discuss in-depth critical issues and trends which are of interest to youths, and to provide the latest updates on exciting developments in China. This year, the forum will focus on three key trends in the ASEAN-China region:

Unlocking AI: Exploring Frontiers of Innovations

Empowering Youth Ambassadors: Bridging Divides in a Diverse World

Wo-men: Deep Dive into the Dynamic World of Women in Tech

This forum will be conducted in both English and Mandarin. Two-way simultaneous interpretation will be provided. Pre-registration for the simultaneous interpretation headset is required for physical attendance. Please indicate accordingly in the registration form.

It is possible to attend the forum in-person or virtually. You may scan the QR codes in the EDM to register accordingly.

Successful registrants will receive a confirmation email after the closing dates on 24 October (for physical attendance) and 26 October (for virtual attendance).

Should you have any other queries, please email to youthforum@businesschina.org.sg.

Consulting is more than giving advice. It extends to providing information, redefining problems, creating targeted solutions — and perhaps beyond that, improving processes. In a nutshell, consulting is about problem-solving, or showing clients the way forward in a particular area of specialisation such as management, finance or operations. Not only then is the consulting industry indispensable, it is also large and diverse, with applications across every sector. That remains true in Singapore. Today, the industry is well-established and recognised as a key driver of Singapore’s professional services sector. Many consulting firms have also partnered with the Singapore Economic Development Board on various endeavours in a bid to drive Singaporean innovation. Looking forward, global trends in sustainability and digitalisation will continue to force organisations to rethink their modus operandi — many have and will thus enlist the help of consulting professionals to enable their transformation. As the industry continues to grow and evolve, demand for professionals is only set to increase.

Interested to find out more about various job roles within the industry and how to best position yourself to join it? Join us online on 24 October for Discover+: Consulting. Sign up to hear from professionals working in the industry!

Greetings from Business China Youth Forum Organising Committee!

The Business China Youth Chapter (BCYC) warmly invites NUS students & staff to participate in the Business China Youth Forum 2023. The title of our upcoming forum is “New Horizons: Trailblazers Shaping the Pathways of Tomorrow”, and we are expecting a diverse group of youths from Singapore, China and the region to discuss up-and-rising opportunities and challenges in our region.

Business China Youth Forum (BCYF) has been organised in tandem with the FutureChina Global Forum (FCGF) since 2019 by the BCYC members. It aims to bring together youths in the ASEAN-China region to discuss in-depth critical issues and trends which are of interest to youths, and to provide the latest updates on exciting developments in China. This year, the forum will focus on three key trends in the ASEAN-China region:

Unlocking AI: Exploring Frontiers of Innovations

Empowering Youth Ambassadors: Bridging Divides in a Diverse World

Wo-men: Deep Dive into the Dynamic World of Women in Tech

This forum will be conducted in both English and Mandarin. Two-way simultaneous interpretation will be provided. Pre-registration for the simultaneous interpretation headset is required for physical attendance. Please indicate accordingly in the registration form.

It is possible to attend the forum in-person or virtually. You may scan the QR codes in the EDM to register accordingly.

Successful registrants will receive a confirmation email after the closing dates on 24 October (for physical attendance) and 26 October (for virtual attendance).

Should you have any other queries, please email to youthforum@businesschina.org.sg.
